2D Lo-Fi Wireframes >> Prototyping ::

In order to redesign the original/reference Oster brand analog toaster-convection oven, I researched many of the competitors with digital user interfaces and functions that the original has.

First, to get an idea of type audience, what they need & want from their toaster oven, I completed an Empathy Map, by Gamestorming.com [link # ] which helped fill some gaps in what I know or think about regarding what folks often cook in these toaster ovens, e.g., is it worth adding a pizza slices and a reheat function? Or, what about toast vs. bagels? One or both?

The key UX method :: a logical and precise decision tree

The implementation of magnetic surfaces is the element in the design that solved the most user experience + interface issues related to using the real-world mockup playing board, pieces and instructions.

This process allowed me to fundementally breakdown every possible task alongside the real analog toaster-oven (my own), with a variety of images, sketches, and examples of digital ones. Which made it easier to stratify, re-order, evaluate, test, and correct. This process also helps separate your project Atomically so it’s easier to distinguish your atoms, molecules, and organisms during the design phase. So, with pencil and graph paper I iterated this phase until I had a logical, user-centric flow, and could be standardized for each main function, e.g., reheat, bake, toast, etc. Eventually, I was able to create a repeatable pattern w/the functions, buttons, and selection dial, which I fine-tuned in the Lo-Fi prototype & wireframes. The Decision Tree was the key to the solution to the analog to digital transformation problems, e.g., how will it work? what will it look like? how big and what order should the buttons be in? what color should the display background be? etc.. And also helped with translating the existing analog oven functions and new ones selected during Research, Discovery, and Empathy Mapping…

For Atomic Results ::

➔ Atomic Mapping Early on was the Key

Problem-Solution Retrospection :: Atomic UX Design was Essential

➔ One of the goals I had with this project was to explore ways to add-in more realism like depth of field, shadows and reflections using metallic textures and material design FX like drop shadow. But, the key to the success of the wireframes and prototyping was applying the concepts of Atomic Design.

➔ For instance, I set the entire “Control Panel Template”/CPT as a Figma Component. But it operates conceptually as an Organism, with the Function Selector Dial and Func Activate Btns as subordinate Molecules & Atoms that can be edited & revised from the main CPT Component and instantly updates all of the wired sub-panels in the Prototype which has about 80 subordinate Components -- this is very powerful & a huge time saver productively.

Making it Work with Atomic Mapping >> again > was key!

➔ Once the process of Mapping and Connecting all of the possible oven operating and cooking features with their corresponding buttons--while also reflecting each mapping of the controls with waht will be displayed on the screen and what user feedback via the buttons, e.g., LED lights on each button face + haptic/touch + audible clicking sounds--will occur. This phase took the longest due to all the combinations between the redesigned buttons, selection dial, functions and each of their sub-functions, e.g., Reheat + Temp + Convect.
